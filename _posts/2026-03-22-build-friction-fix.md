just start.

i had to just start somewhere. building, writing, engaging.

## build

take one step. there's no perfect prompt. no perfect config. no perfect preconditions.

code or research. analyzing data or jotting down ideas. it doesn't matter where.

this applies at every level — a single implementation, a larger feature, a whole system, across projects, across your entire thinking.

take one step now.

## friction

as you work, name what's not working.

what didn't go like you wanted? what was harder than you thought?

be explicit. "here's the friction." say it out loud.

## fix

flip into fix.

ask claude, opencode, codex, whatever tool exists by the time you read this — "here's the friction i had. how can i fix that moving forward?"

maybe the fix is in how you're framing it. it's human prompting after all — the way you say things matters.

maybe it's structural. update your claude md, project notes, memory, a skill, a hook, a command. leverage the primitives.

and in conjunction — think for yourself. what do i think this is? how do i think this will be solved? how might it be solved?

compare your thinking to the tool's recommendation. this is where you start to understand the shape of the overall system — how things are interacting, how you think, how the tools think.

then fix it. right now.

## the loop

build. friction. fix. repeat.

the patterns emerge. the shape of the work, the shape of how you work. you're building your system.

if this sounds familiar — it's basically a retro. "how did it go? what can we try?" there's nothing new here.

it's just how i'm using these tools and patterns.

happy building.
