## the drowning

every message looks the same with the same structure the same tone the same bullet points followed by the same sign-off.

meeting summaries reading like a press release from a company that sells nothing. three bajillion emojis. rocket ship. pointing finger. sparkles. megaphone. check check check

walls of text. enormous blocks of formulaically formatted absolutely soulless text. paragraphs that say everything and mean nothing. every word chosen for probabilistically perfection for this context this channel this audience.

woo, we're optimized... but for what? clicks, comments, views... get more eyeballs. always more eyeballs. YOU ARE ENGAGEMENT

linkedin, slack, "probabilistically and algorithmically personalized emails."

"i told my team something vulnerable and learned a powerful lesson." "i did X and here's what i learned about modern <insert_your_specific_business_function_that_nobody_gives_a_fuck_about_probabilistically_speaking>." "i failed publicly and here's why that's actually a superpower." the same post, rearranged, posted by ten thousand different people who all somehow had the same revelation on the same tuesday. the engagement farms liking each other's stuff, commenting, sycophantically synergistic smattering shillery. comments disguised as _the_ perfect opportunity for someone else's posts.

optimized for the most bland, boring-ass, milquetoast, sterile WINNING CRUSHING IT OH THE CHART IS UP AND TO THE RIGHT I'M GONNA BE RICH AND ABLE TO BUY EVERYONE'S LOVE AND ADORATION YES.

phew... is this authenticity... is this what we aspire to? ooooooooof.

i can't unsee it. feel the walls closing in, give me your blandness be consumed submit. write, create, connect: this opposition and tension is fuel :)

## gonzo

hunter s. thompson didn't write gonzo because he wanted to be weird. he wrote it because the journalism of his era had become so sanitized, so careful, so committed to the illusion of objectivity that it couldn't capture what was actually happening. the conventions were lies. the "balanced" reporting was a kind of cowardice. so he blew it up. Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas. on the campaign trail. the Kentucky Derby piece that remains, decades later, one of the most alive things ever written about a sporting event.

i found thompson in college. started reading him back when he was at ESPN (lol this cracks me up... imagining someone pitching this in 2026... ha!). the surface-level stuff. then Fear and Loathing and the doors came off. this is possible? you can do this? and then the letters, the campaign reporting, the full body of work. a person who wrote like his life depended on it because he understood that when the pressure to conform gets high enough, the only honest response is to refuse. not to be provocative for its own sake. to be real when real has become the radical act.

the conditions create the response. when conformity is enforced, whether by algorithms or authoritarian impulse or the gentle pressure of professional norms, authentic creative expression doesn't just become more valuable. it becomes necessary. this isn't new. this pattern has a history longer than any of us. and right now? the bland convergence is the conformity. everything racing to the most professionally acceptable, algorithmically optimized, engagement-friendly version of nothing. the soul got optimized out and nobody noticed because the formatting was so clean.

and yeah, i see the irony. this post has sections. it has a structure. i'm using tools to write it. that's not the problem. the problem is formula as a substitute for thinking. "what's the 5 step plan to implement someone else's system" instead of "what the hell do i actually think?" using craft to organize your actual thoughts is not the same as using a template to avoid having thoughts.
