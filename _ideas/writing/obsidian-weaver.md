## the kernel

another workflow i use. claude code as the interface to my obsidian vault, skills as the verbs, and a background script that weaves connections between notes as i write them. the knowledge graph wasn't the plan. it emerged.

repo: https://github.com/marshallhouston/cosmic-farmland (plugins/obsidian-weaver). lives alongside the other cosmic-farmland plugins, not a standalone thing.

## fleshed out design (2026-04-17)

### register / energy

same spine as `tools-repo-tour.md`. measured, explanatory, showing-the-workflow. "hey, here's another workflow i use." not a product launch. not a pitch. no demand argument. the workflow has intrinsic value. let it do the talking.

### audience

obsidian users who want less manual linking. claude code users building their own plugins. people who've tried "second brain" systems and bounced off the ceremony.

### post arc

#### 1. the open
short. "another workflow i use." name the shape in one breath:
- claude code is the interface.
- obsidian is the filesystem and visualizer.
- skills are the verbs.
- a background script weaves the graph.

repo link drops here. no throat-clearing.

#### 2. the three skills
quick table or list. one line each.
- `/today` — daily note. calendar sync, carryover, meeting stubs.
- `/capture` — brain dump or url in, structured note out, auto-tagged, connections found.
- `/connect-sync` — scans recently modified notes, weaves the graph.

this is the verb surface. everything else is convention.

#### 3. why i built it (the BFF arc)
started with `/today`. then `/capture`. used them. noticed i kept typing the same person's name across different notes and wanting them linked. `/connect-sync` is the fix for that friction. the knowledge graph wasn't in the original plan. it emerged because i kept writing and the friction kept showing up in the same place.

link to the BFF post here.

#### 4. a day in the loop
lift the king henry / BBNNN / platform migration slice from the README. tighten it. this is the heart of the post — show it, don't describe it. the reader sees:
- morning `/today` framing the day
- 1:1 notes landing in the right place
- `/capture` while walking back from coffee
- strategy doc work
- afternoon `/connect-sync` reporting the weave

#### 5. the compound
one week in: 8 linked source notes to a theme i never manually linked. one month in: 31. the graph view shows clusters you didn't design — projects dense at the middle, people as spokes, themes as bridges. you wrote. it wove.

the second-order stuff is the payoff: overlap between a philosophical thread (BBNNN) and a concrete project (platform migration) shows up quantitatively. your stated priorities and your actual time leave a trail.

#### 6. close
seeds, not products. starting point. BFF it for yourself. the repo is there. rename, delete, extend. the three skills are the ones i hit friction with first. yours will be different.
