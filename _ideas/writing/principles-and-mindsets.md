## principles

### action brings clarity
lead with doing, not theorizing. build, don't plan forever. the learning is in the action.

"action brings clarity. stop starting, start finishing."

### depth over breadth
durable learning comes from going deep, not skimming the surface. resist the pull to try everything. go deep on fewer things.

### when it feels possible to build everything, it doesn't mean we should build everything possible
the constraint principle. just because ai makes building cheap doesn't mean everything is worth building. restraint is a skill.

### compounding systems
build things that get better over time. incident journals, documentation, automation — each use makes the next one easier. "every interaction improves the system for next time."

### observable by default
if you can't see it, you can't improve it. instrument everything. make the work visible. this applies to code, to teams, to personal practice.

### intentional architecture
don't let things sprawl. make deliberate choices about structure. the opposite of "just throw it in there." applies to code and to how teams organize work.

### experiment freely, optimize later
don't optimize prematurely. try things. the cambrian explosion metaphor — we're in a burst of experimentation. strategy is variety and selection, not planning and predicting. optimize for number and diversity of experiments, don't converge too early.

## mindsets

### fitfo (figure it the f out)
try things. don't wait for permission. don't wait for the perfect prompt, the perfect tool, the perfect plan. "there is no perfect prompt — iterate, learn, adjust."

### comfort with ambiguity
not everything needs to be resolved before you move. sit with the uncertainty and act anyway. "i am on the precipice, and yet i must still act."

### updating priors
be willing to change your mind. what was true six months ago might not be true now. openness to change is a mindset, not a one-time event.

### constraints as creative force
narrow what's possible, discover what matters. the cooking analogy — salt to over-salting, then dial back. "constraints and maximalism are two sides of the same coin."

### the experiment is the thing
everything else is secondary. the experimentation budget framework: how do i increase capacity? how do i decrease marginal cost? where am i right now? sustainability matters — periodization, not burnout.

## frameworks that connect to these

### bff (build, friction, fix)
the fractal pattern. start with blank slate, try things, build, identify friction, fix, rinse and repeat. same shape at every scale — individual, team, org.

### fractals
same shape at every scale: clarify → iterate → verify → incorporate feedback → improve the system. scale-invariant fundamentals.

### the arc
data → knowledge → insights → action. the progression from raw information to meaningful impact.

### the moving boundary
the line between what humans do and what ai does keeps shifting. the principles above are about navigating that shift, not fighting it.

### waterline principle (decision-making)
you have authority to make decisions — it depends on role, scope, impact. above waterline = your team's responsibility. push decisions down. don't undermine decisions above your waterline.

## leadership-specific principles

### default stance: empowerment
four functions of a manager: mentor, coach, sponsor, provide feedback (lara hogan).

### rock in the lake, not a dam
create ripples, don't block flow. influence through presence, not control.

### keep the main thing the main thing
from bryce young. focus and finish. don't let the shiny thing pull you off course.

### first team mindset
your first team is your peer group, not your direct reports. represent the business.

### celebrate effectiveness, not activity
"if we celebrate activity (prs, lines of code), then we'll get more activity. how do we celebrate effectiveness?"
