a framework for thinking about experimentation as a finite resource to manage.

## core idea

every experiment has a cost. opportunity cost, cognitive cost, marginal cost. everyone has a budget. same experiment, vastly different drawdown depending on your capacity and cost. how to increase your overall capacity, how to decrease the marginal cost of each experiment, and how to know where you are on your budget right now.

## why this matters

the pace of change demands constant experimentation. new tools, new patterns, new ways of working every week. but "just experiment more" without a sustainability model leads to burnout. we need a framework for experimentation that's as rigorous as our frameworks for delivery.

## analogies

draws on endurance sports, formula one, and olympic periodization. you don't go hard all the time; you build cycles.

## fractal nature

the framework applies at the individual, team, and organizational level.

## audience takeaway

a practical lens for sustainable experimentation in a world that demands constant learning.
