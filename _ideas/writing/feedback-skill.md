## the kernel

the feedback skill. section-by-section review page, fill it in, paste it back, claude applies. use it on drafts. use it on synthesized docs. use it on app ideas sitting in `_ideas/`. basically anywhere i have words on a page and want to react to them with structure instead of vibes.

not a tool post. a workflow post. the shape of thinking it unlocks.

## threads to pull

- what the skill actually is: `/feedback <file>` -> `generate-feedback-html` -> browser -> markdown back -> applied. show the flow.
- why section-by-section beats conversation. conversation feedback drifts. structured feedback has a place to live.
- "instead x -> do y" as a gesture. one move, not three.
- the surprise: it's not just for drafts. it works on anything that has sections. tool ideas. meeting synth. app pitches. brainstorm dumps.
- the meta-move: using it on non-writing makes you realize all thinking is writing eventually.
- how it changes the altitude of your feedback. the form forces you to react at the right level per section instead of globally hand-waving.
- the handoff: you give reactions, claude carries them into the doc. you don't have to hold both roles at once.
- connection to writing-process post — this is the tool that made that pipeline work. but this post is about the skill itself, not the pipeline.
- connection to wispr flow: voice into the feedback boxes. talk your reactions, don't type them.
- what it replaces: freeform "hey claude what do you think" loops that produced loose, globally-scoped edits.
