## the kernel

grief and mourning as ai-augmented software engineering rapidly changes the nature of the work. a sense of loss and drift that keeps showing up in conversations -- and not from people resisting AI, but from people deep in it.

## threads to pull

- who is feeling this? people using AI tools all the time, not people avoiding them
- loss and drift as specific emotional textures -- what exactly is being mourned? craft? identity? the feeling of knowing what you're doing?
- coping strategies, tactics, skills -- what are people actually doing? what works?
- how do you move toward a future that is genuinely different while processing the emotions of the transition?
- writing to process. bids for connection. talking to people. what are the healthy forms this takes?
- future research: this is going to be someone's master's thesis or PhD. what will they find? what will they ask?
- what are the impacts? what does the data look like? what are people doing?
- the speed of the change as its own variable -- is grief proportional to velocity?
- community and connection as part of the coping -- not just individual processing

## marshall's reflections (2026-04-04)

i keep running into this in conversations. the people feeling it aren't the ones who refuse to use AI -- they're the ones using it every day. something about being inside the change makes the loss more acute, not less.

this feels important and underexplored. somebody is going to write the definitive research on this period. i want to be part of naming it before that happens.
