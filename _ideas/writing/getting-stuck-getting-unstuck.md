the idea that getting stuck isn't failure — it's the actual job. and getting unstuck is the skill.

## origin

from a railsconf talk, way back: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3XscuivvUzI

zach lamb credited me with this idea — said he learned it from me. that it was a key piece of how i showed up. which is wild and affirming to hear reflected back.

## core idea

everyone gets stuck. that's not the differentiator. the differentiator is what you get stuck on and how you move forward from it. junior engineers get stuck on syntax. senior engineers get stuck on system design. staff engineers get stuck on organizational dynamics. the stuckness never goes away — it just changes shape.

## why this is empowering

reframing stuckness from "i'm failing" to "this is the work" is freeing. it normalizes the discomfort. it gives people permission to be where they are. and it shifts the focus from avoiding difficulty to building the muscle of getting unstuck.
