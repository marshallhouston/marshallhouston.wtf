## fleshed out design (2026-04-01)

### register/energy

high energy mania. gonzo-adjacent, inspired by hunter s. thompson. not performing thompson but channeling that same "i have to respond to this" urgency. the mania is pressure-release: creativity compressed by bland culture finding its outlet. declarative conviction woven through the rant (borrowing from manifesto energy).

### audience

for me first. always. bids for connection, creating community, inviting others in. people who might find it: other writers, tech folks who haven't thought about using ai this way, people new to tools like claude code for creative work beyond just code. but not writing to any of those audiences specifically.

### framing

approach a (the rant that opens into something) with manifesto conviction woven in. the frustration is the hook, and it's honest. the historical thread stays light here (a beat, not a survey). deeper sensemaking on the historical pattern is a future post.

the biographical creative arc (alabama documentaries, photography exhibit, student newspaper, 15-20 years of creative life resurfacing) is fuel underneath the post, not content in it. the reader feels the weight of someone who means it. future post can flesh that out and link back.

### post arc

#### 1. the drowning
the entry. you're in it. linkedin. slack. ai summaries. everything racing to the most boring middle. sterile formula optimized for engagement, likes, comments. so optimized for the metric that the soul disappeared. no preamble, no scene-setting. the reader is already underwater with you.

job: make the reader feel the suffocation. they recognize it because they live in it too.

tone: frustrated, visceral, fast. not explaining, experiencing.

#### 2. the diagnosis
pull back just enough to name what's happening. convergence. ai-augmented writing, seo optimization, linkedin formulas, algorithmic engagement. all racing toward the mean. not because any individual is bad, but because the systems reward sameness. result: authenticity becomes scarce.

the deeper cut: "are you there? are you human?" robots talking to robots, agents interfacing with agents, the actual person disappeared. it's not just that everything sounds the same. it's that we can't tell if anyone is actually there anymore. slop and emojis and summaries, no one engaged with me directly. lose all personal soul authenticity.

job: turn the feeling from section 1 into a named thing. "bland convergence" gets its moment. the scarcity framing lands here.

tone: still high energy but sharpening. from "fuck this" to "here's what's actually happening."

#### 3. the historical beat
brief. not the full sensemaking (future post). a beat that says: this isn't new. art has always responded to conformity. thompson didn't write gonzo because he wanted to be weird. he wrote it because the times demanded something real. the conditions cause people to respond in different ways. when pressure to conform increases, authentic creative expression becomes the resistance. one or two references, not a survey. just enough to anchor that this is a pattern, not a tantrum.

job: legitimize the energy. this isn't just frustration. this is what creators do when the conditions demand it.

tone: still moving fast, but a moment of grounding. the post takes a breath here before accelerating again.

#### 4. the declaration
stop diagnosing, start declaring. authenticity is where deep connection lives. one degree off normal. the weird, the real, the thing that makes someone go "oh, that's a person." not provocative for shock value. irreverence with substance. you're a creative person. you've always been one. and this is your response to the current moment. the post itself is the proof.

the invitation is embedded throughout, not a separate section. bids for connection are woven into the whole piece, not saved for a closing bow. the post ends here, with conviction. facing forward.

job: stake the claim. not arguing for authenticity, embodying it.

tone: conviction. the mania channels into something clear and forward-leaning. "this is what i believe and i'm doing it."
