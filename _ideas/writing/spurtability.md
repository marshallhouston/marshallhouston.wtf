Spurtability. A rapid period of intensity -- roughly 2 hours -- and then a nap.

## additional threads (2026-04-04)

the patterns:
- waking up in the middle of the night, wired. get the creativity out. max two hours. weighted eye mask. right back to sleep.
- this happens at different points in the day, not just at night -- same pattern, different time slot
- rest -> creative intensity -> recover. that's the full cycle, and all three parts are the work.

two modes: on or off. nothing in between. when it's time to go, it's go. understanding and accepting this is the whole thing.

the discipline question: how do you work with this, not against it?
- create the conditions when you feel it coming
- be open to it when it arrives unexpectedly
- be willing to quit when it's not there. stopping is the discipline, not pushing through.
- not setting the boundary burns the mental experimentation budget. burnout is the tax on ignoring the signal.

the weighted eye mask as a tool, not a quirk. signals to the body: spurt is over, recovery begins now.

can't force it. the question is: are the conditions right? am i open? and can i recognize when it's time to stop?
