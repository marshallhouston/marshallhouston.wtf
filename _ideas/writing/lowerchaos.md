# lowerchaos

the lowercase thing is deliberate. it's not ai-generated slop, it's my voice. i've been writing lowercase since before llms existed. but feedback made it clear: people spend more energy on "why is this all lowercase" than on the actual ideas. the style becomes a barrier to the content.

## threads

- lowercase as identity pushback. em dashes used to be my thing, now they're "oh ai wrote this." lowercase is the same fight. "this is ME."
- the tension between self-expression and accessibility. i like lowercase. but if it stops people from engaging with what i'm actually saying, what's the point?
- building the capitalize toggle as a response. not removing lowercase — it's still the default. but offering a choice. "here, read it however you want, now can we talk about the ideas?"
- "lowerchaos" as a name for the aesthetic. owning it.
- the broader question: when does stylistic choice become friction? when do you hold the line vs adapt?

## fleshed out design (2026-04-05)

### post arc
#### 1. the tension
the jarring is deliberate. one degree off normal. that's the creative impulse, the whole point of lowercase.

#### 2. the compromise
but people i respect kept snagging on the style instead of the ideas. jesse, jeff, an engineer on the team. at some point the bit is getting in the way. so it's a creative cost, but the people matter more than the bit.

#### 3. the response
built a toggle. lowercase is still the default. but the option is there.

#### 4. the closer
brief identity claim. it's still mine though. the lowercase isn't going anywhere.

### register/energy
quiet/minimal. feedback responsiveness. no defensiveness, no manifesto. just: here's what happened, here's what i did, it's still mine.

### audience
people coming from the linkedin thread or who already know the site is lowercase. doesn't need to stand alone for cold readers.

### additional notes
- reference the toggle, don't show it. let people discover it.

- the "cost" angle is key: this wasn't free, it was a compromise, and that's what makes it real.
