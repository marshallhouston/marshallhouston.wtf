## fleshed out design (2026-04-01)

### framing

AI-augmented writing process shown in action. inspired by ethan mollick's co-intelligence ideas. not a tutorial, not persuasion. "here's what i'm doing, come along if you want." bid for connection, community building. the process improving itself is proof the process works.

clarity of thinking and authenticity are key. leverage tools. share approach with others.

standalone post. connected to the authenticity kernel but distinct. this is the "how it works" post, authenticity is the "why it matters" post.

### register/energy

measured, process-focused, MEB-adjacent. "let me show you how this works." show don't tell. visually rich throughout: notebook photos, screenshots, screen recordings at each stage.

### audience

primarily for me, to capture current flow and see how it evolves over time. shared openly for anyone interested. not trying to convince anyone of anything. culture creator, community builder.

### pipeline (the thing being shown)

notebook -> kernel -> idea -> refine (why, what, what do i want people to feel) -> draft -> revision cycles at different levels -> actual words exactly -> published post. happens over days or weeks. ideas emerge, fluctuate, breathe, find out what exactly to convey from ideas and language.

the refine step between idea and draft is key: Q&A focused on the why, what you're trying to accomplish, the emotions or feelings you want others to feel. don't get caught in details of language too early.

#### 1. opening / entry point
mollick's co-intelligence as the spark. AI isn't a replacement for thinking, it's a thinking partner. then the pivot: "i've been living this for the past few weeks. here's what the process actually looks like." brief, grounding. not a book report on mollick, just the thread that connects to what follows. tone: measured, invitational. "come along if you want."

#### 2. the pipeline overview
full map before zooming in. notebook -> kernel -> idea -> refine (why, what, feelings) -> draft -> revision cycles -> published post. quick, visual. "here's the shape of the thing, now let me show you how each of these has evolved."

#### 3. the BFF era (march 21-22)
first version of the AI-augmented loop. claude code on the left, locally served blog post on the right, wispr flow for voice feedback. back and forth: read the post, talk through what's not working, bring that into claude code, new draft, look again. overall structure feedback, big-picture stuff. "is the arc right? does this flow?"

what you learned: the loop works, but feedback was loose. hard to distinguish "rewrite this section" from "change this specific phrase." everything lived in conversation.

recreate this flow with screenshots/screen recording. will be slightly retroactive but that's fine. want to show what it actually looked like.

tone: retrospective but alive. "this is where it started."

#### 4. the MEB evolution
first big system improvement. friction from BFF: overall feedback and specific wording feedback getting tangled. built review.html with structured feedback sections directly in the doc. feedback now had a place to live. revision cycles got more layered: first passes on structure and voice, later passes on exact language.

wispr flow thread: voice keeps you at the right altitude for the stage you're in. typing pulls you into wordsmithing too early. talking is much faster than typing. don't worry about formatting. keeps focus on the "right" thing for this stage instead of chasing rabbit holes, losing focus, spending limited mental focus on the wrong thing at the wrong time. "keep the main thing the main thing." wispr flow as cognitive routing, not just a convenience tool. feels more like me.

the shift: from "conversation as feedback" to "structured feedback with a dedicated tool."

screenshots showing the evolution.

#### 5. the current system (this post)
review.html with highlighting, "instead X -> do Y" flow. keeps specific wording feedback precise without rewriting entire sections. skills and commands to make the review cycle repeatable. screen recording of this in action on this actual post.

evolution complete: loose conversation feedback -> structured feedback sections -> highlighting with direct substitution guidance. each layer came from friction in the previous one.

tone: "and here's where it is today." satisfaction without finality. the system will keep evolving.

#### 6. the invitation / close
jeff casimir as a concrete example of someone else doing this their own way. ruby to python with AI as coach and collaborator. same principle: AI as thinking partner, not replacement. shows this isn't a solo thing.

## refine (2026-04-01)

### why this post, why now?
because showing people how i'm using AI-augmented writing gets the "oh shit i didn't know that was possible" reaction. that moment is the thing driving this. not documenting for future me, not persuading anyone. i'm fascinated by what i've built and want to share that fascination.

### what am i trying to accomplish?
show my process and its evolution honestly enough that it sparks the reader's own ideas. not a template to copy, but proof that a deeply personal version exists, which gives permission to build their own. the evolution matters because it shows this grew from friction, making it accessible rather than intimidating. walkthrough energy is fine, just with the right register: a tour, not a lecture. layer depth so people can skim or dive.

### what do i want readers to feel?
wonder. "what might be possible for me?" not impressed by the system, not convinced of anything. opened up, curious about their own version of this.
