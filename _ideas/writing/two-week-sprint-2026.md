examining what agile was actually solving and what the equivalent looks like when ai changes the cost of building.

## core idea

the two-week sprint and the agile manifesto emerged from a specific historical moment. early 2000s, waterfall bloat, long feedback cycles. that context has shifted. what problems was agile actually solving? what's the 2026 equivalent? what's different now and what's stayed the same?

## why this matters

we're at a similar inflection point to the one that produced the agile manifesto. the cost of building has fundamentally changed, but many of our processes haven't caught up. this is the right time to ask what we're carrying forward because it works vs. what we're carrying forward out of habit.

## key thread

the fundamentals of good software development (clarifying thinking, small batches, feedback loops, building what matters) are still here. it's new, but it's not that new.

## audience takeaway

questioning inherited practices and thinking about what principles should guide how we work now.
