# cambrian explosion and fractals

talk script + beat sheet. 30min, ~6 beats, 2min buffer.

audience: entire technology org. higher-level, not domain-deep.

close (a/b/c) intentionally left open until later passes.

opening posture. not "here's the answer." here's how i own my understanding.

talking points:
- one way i own understanding: analogies. reason about them. see what fits, where it breaks.
- today, two analogies for what's happening in our industry: cambrian explosion + fractals.
- yes, stretch. come along. cosmic farmland. we're making connections, seeing what we can learn.
- not airtight. if you think i'm full of shit, tell me. then we get to learn together.

## beat 2 — cambrian, what it is (5min)

brief. not scientific reading. enough to anchor the metaphor.

talking points:
- ~540M years ago. sudden burst. most major body plans appear.
- not gradual. not predicted. environment shifted, variety exploded.
- three ideas worth borrowing:
  - we don't know what will be successful. nobody did then either.
  - combinations succeed where singletons don't. remix. recombination.
  - many bets. asymmetric upside. most die. the few that compound define the era.

slide: simple diagram/image of timeline, "body plans explode."

---

## beat 3 — cambrian, applied (5min)

bridge metaphor → industry posture. keep abstract (per tight timeline, no specific examples).

talking points:
- rapid change right now. environment shifted. tools, capabilities, expectations.
- can't predict winners. nobody can. stop trying to converge early.
- portfolio posture: breadth > precision. place many bets. kill them off cheap.
- remix > invention. combinations of existing pieces win.
- where it bends: extinction in nature is free. our bets cost attention, budget, trust. so: cheap bets, fast kills.

slide: "variety + selection" / "place bets, kill them cheap."

## beat 4 — fractals, what they are (4min)

love letter. short.

talking points:
- repeating pattern at different scales.
- zoom in, zoom out, shape is the same.
- in nature: coastlines, trees, lungs, rivers.
- useful because: if you see the shape once, you can recognize it everywhere.

slide: fractal image (coastline or tree).

## beat 5 — fractals, three scales in our work (7min)

three axes. walk them.

### 5a. feedback loops (tight scale)
- repl / test / pr / release / postmortem.
- same shape: try → observe → adjust.
- what changes across the loops is latency and stakes. shape doesn't.

### 5b. time scale (decades)
- grady booch: "golden age of software." we've been here before.
- mainframe → pc → web → mobile → ai.
- each wave: explosion of variety, consolidation, platform, next explosion.
- repetition on time scale. pattern recognizable if you zoom out.

### 5c. people scale (widening)
- problem/why → solve → feedback → ship → learn.
- runs at: story. epic. feature. initiative. team. org. company.
- same dance, widening circle.
- implication: practices that work at one scale likely work at others. and failures at one scale forecast failures at the others.

slide: 3 scales stacked. same shape shown at each.

## beat 6. close (4min). let it burn.

learn from nature. learn from our own mistake with it.

talking points:
- us forest service, early 1900s: fight every fire. suppress everything. feels responsible.
- decades of suppression, fuel load stacks up. understory thickens. dead wood accumulates.
- eventually you don't get small fires anymore. you get catastrophic ones. yellowstone '88. paradise '18. etc.
- nature was already running a process. small fires clear fuel, recycle nutrients, open canopy, trigger seed germination (some pinecones only open in fire).
- policy finally updated. prescribed burns. let some fires run. nature knew what it was doing.
- us in tech. same pattern.
  - we suppress. we keep every project alive. we prop up things past their usefulness. we can't say no, can't kill, can't sunset.
  - fuel stacks up. cognitive load. maintenance burden. distraction. team capacity drained by things nobody would start today.
  - the catastrophic fire is the reorg, the layoff, the collapse of a product line under its own weight.
- the move: intellectual honesty. look at what's there. admit what should burn. let it.
- this closes the loop with cambrian. you can't have the explosion of variety without extinction. the winners only compound if the losers get out of the way.
- fractals of this: task level (kill a branch). project level (sunset a service). team level (stop staffing it). org level (end the initiative). same shape. same discipline. same honesty.

closing line candidates:
- "nature knew. we have to relearn it."
- "the fire isn't the failure. suppressing it is."
- "intellectual honesty, or the fire picks for you."

slide: "let it burn." fire/regrowth imagery.
