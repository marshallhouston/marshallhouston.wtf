ptvm. prove the value motherfucker.

is this actually doing anything or nah. is this valuable or fluff. specific tests. specific processes. specific changes. the gut check you run before you believe your own hype. run it on anything. run it on this post.

## 532 tests passing

personal project. `532 tests passing`. wait, what the fuck. how do we have this many tests already. think of kent beck.

claude's in the chat: "hey, great job! you've got soooo many tests you're such a good lil developer" pats my back. i pat claude's back. we simultaneously fart and ahhh, hmmm, going back to the wine and chocolate and cheese pairing groupon experience we had in 2012, swirl around in mouth, what's that i'm getting... hmmm is it dior? giorgio? middle school pe locker room of BRO CHECK THIS OUT I GOT AXE TO COVER UP MY B.O. PEOPLE WILL LOVE ME NOW smell? can of freshly opened tennis balls?

self-congratulatory bullshit.

beck said this already. tests as coins you collect, not asking what each one is actually worth. the suite grew because adding tests felt like doing the work. green bar felt like doing the work. neither one is the same as value.

ptvm on every test. does this catch a real failure mode. or is it a coin.

## the slop

then minh pham's post hits the feed.

> I'm really annoyed with this trend around sub-agents, and agent teams, and multi-tasking the crap out of anything and everything we can.
>
> It's like we've forgotten that multi-tasking is generally not a productive practice. I think the worst thing to come from AI in recent months has been this normalization of poor focus. I see businesses, teams, engineers running off to do as many things as humanly possible leaving behind this trail of broken, busted, partly working slop.
>
> I get it, its hard to ignore - its like tiktok or any other short-form content. It preys on our need for dopamine and instant gratification. I feel like it took awhile before we realized what tiktok style content was doing to people's attention spans. I hope tech doesn't come to realize a similar fate with AI tools too down the line....

this is [probabilistic perfect piggies]({% post_url 2026-04-03-probabilistically-perfect-piggies %}). every sub-agent a fresh piggy. every broken-busted-partly-working thing another coin on the pile. the slop isn't a side effect. it's the output we're congratulating ourselves for.

ptvm on every agent spawn. ptvm on every sub-task. does this thing do anything or are we just piggy-stacking.

busyness mistaking itself for impact.

## why that, why now

zoom out again. product roadmaps. features shipping. iterations queued. everyone busy.

the question nobody puts on the doc. why this. why that. why now. what breaks if we don't.

demand is assumed. signal is vibes. the roadmap is a coin pile dressed up in a gantt chart. a whole industry mistaking velocity for value. shipping as self-congratulation. standup as the green bar.

ptvm on the roadmap. ptvm on the feature. ptvm on the iteration.
