just start.

## build

take one step. there's no perfect prompt. no perfect config. no perfect preconditions.

whether you start with code or research. a daily task analyzing data, jotting down ideas, seeing what happens. it doesn't matter where. take one step now.

this applies at every level — a single implementation, a larger feature, a whole system, across projects, across your entire thinking.

## friction

as you work, identify friction points. what didn't go like you wanted? what was harder than you thought?

name it explicitly. "here's the friction." be specific about it.

## fix

flip into fix. ask claude, opencode, codex, whatever tool exists by the time you read this — "here's the friction i had. how can i fix that moving forward?"

maybe the fix is in how you're framing it — it's human prompting after all, the way you say things matters. or maybe the fix is structural — update your claude md, project notes, memory, a skill, a hook, a command. leverage the primitives.

then literally fix it. right now.

## do the thinking yourself

how might i fix this? what do i think the recommendation will be and why?

this is the part that matters. you're building your own understanding — of how you think, how these tools think. you start to see the shape of it.

## the loop

now keep doing it. build, friction, fix.

the patterns emerge. the shape of the work, the shape of how you work. you're building your system.

and if this sounds familiar — slash insights, summarize, review, apply — it's basically a retro. "how did it go? didn't go well. what can we try?" there's nothing new here. it's just how i'm leveraging these tools and patterns.

happy building.
